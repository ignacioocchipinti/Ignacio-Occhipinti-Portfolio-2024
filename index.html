(About me)

My name is Ignacio, but everyone calls me Nacho. I was born on December 11, 1998, so if my math is correct I'm 25 years old.

Passionate about the audiovisual world, after high school, I opted for a career in Multimedia Arts at the National University of Arts where, without knowing, I met development world too, and I loved it! In 2023, I got my Associate Degree in Multimedia Arts and now I am preparing to get my Bachelor Degree.

I work as an E-commerce Cataloging Analyst at SEIDOR Argentina where I've learned lot of data management and user experience. Although my previous roles were mainly in customer service, these experiences have provided me with skills in interpersonal relationships, problem solving and efficient and quality decision making so I can say that working in group is a natural thing for me.

Why should you get in contact with me? Because I am passionate about learning, and my expectations for obtaining a position in the design/development world are to absorb everything it has to offer me, challenging myself in pursuit of achieving my best version and expanding my experience and knowledge. I always prioritize ensuring that it is a beneficial experience for both me and you, performing to the best of my ability and helping with everything within my reach.

PALAN'T

Video game with motion capture based on light tracking and physics libraries developed in Processing. The game's narrative is based on the figure of a politician whose goal is to collect money while avoiding shovels that metaphorically represent the act of working. Additionally, the politician will be hindered by bills that slow down their movements. It is an endless game; as certain amounts of money are collected, the difficulty increases, and the player who surpasses the most difficulty levels is the winner.

Processing

Nowacki voice creator

This is a program developed in p5.js that, based on audio capture and its analysis in terms of frequency and amplitude, replicates works created by the artist Andrzej Nowacki. At the beginning, you choose which work of the artist you want to replicate. Then, based on the amplitude, each part is drawn, and based on the frequency used, colors are selected. Finally, your final work can be downloaded.
